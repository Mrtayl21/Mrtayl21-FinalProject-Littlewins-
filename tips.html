Classroom Tips

These quick tips help new teachers feel more confident and consistent. Pick one strategy to try this week.

Tip 1: Teach routines like lessons

Don’t assume students “know” what lining up or turning in work looks like. Model the routine, practice it, and give quick feedback.

  • Model it once
  • Practice 2–3 times
  • Praise what you want repeated

Tip 2: Be consistent (even when you’re tired)

Consistency builds trust and reduces power struggles. Use the same language for reminders and follow through with the same steps.

Example script: “First directions, then questions.”

Tip 3: Celebrate small wins

Notice growth—even tiny growth. A student who started work in 2 minutes instead of 10 made progress. Celebrate it.

Tip 4: Keep materials predictable

Predictable systems reduce chaos. Keep turn-in trays, pencil supplies, and daily agenda in the same place.

One-minute reset

If the class is off-track, pause and reset calmly: regain attention, restate directions, and try the transition again. Resets are normal—and they work.
